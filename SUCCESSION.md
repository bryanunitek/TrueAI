# Succession and Stewardship

**Source-of-truth location for the named successor to the UniVERSE / TrueAI / UniCORE AI programme.**

This document is the canonical place where succession arrangements for the programme are recorded. It is referenced from the TrueAI Foundation whitepaper (`docs/whitepaper/WHITEPAPER.md` in the [TrueAI repository](https://github.com/bryanunitek/TrueAI)) and from the Governed Intelligence whitepaper (`docs/whitepaper/WHITEPAPER.md` in this repository).

---

## Why this document exists

A foundation intended to outlast any single author or organisation requires a succession statement. The CC BY 4.0 licence preserves the documents and the right to cite, translate, adapt, and build upon them independently of any future stewardship. Stewardship determines who may issue revisions on behalf of the programme; it does not determine whether the programme itself continues to exist.

The gift principle, once given, is not subject to succession. No future steward can revoke it.

---

## Succession statement (v1.0)

> In the event the principal author is unable to continue, stewardship devolves to a named successor nominated by Bryan Fred and recorded in the UniVERSE repository. Failing such nomination, to Unitek Systems Limited, which shall designate a successor by public process within 12 months.

This statement is the standing arrangement for the UniVERSE programme and for its component foundations (TrueAI and UniCORE AI). It is reproduced verbatim in the TrueAI whitepaper, section 8.

## What succession does and does not transfer

**Succession transfers:**

- Authority to issue revisions to the canonical documents (UniVERSE foundation series, TrueAI whitepaper, UniCORE AI whitepaper and architecture)
- Authority to publish version updates and record them in each document's revision appendix
- Authority to accept community contributions through GitHub Discussions on behalf of the programme
- Authority to designate further successors using the procedure recorded in this document

**Succession does not transfer:**

- The licence (CC BY 4.0 is irrevocable and held by no one)
- The gift principle (it has already been given)
- The right to enclose, paywall, or commercialise the foundation itself
- The right to retract or alter previously published canonical versions (those remain in the repository's git history under the licence as originally published)

---

## Public process (fallback)

If Bryan Fred does not name a successor before becoming unable to continue, Unitek Systems Limited shall designate a successor by **public process within 12 months**. The public process shall, at minimum:

1. Be announced through the GitHub Discussions of all five public repositories (UniVERSE, TrueAI, UniCORE-AI, UniCORE, UniCORE.GVB).
2. Solicit nominations from the research, standards, and adopter communities.
3. Apply a clear, published rubric for selection — including respect for the gift principle, the Nine Invariants, and the programme's stated independence from any single commercial entity.
4. Record the final designation in this document by amendment under the same git-history-preserved process used for all programme changes.

Unitek Systems Limited may delegate the operation of the public process to an independent body, provided that body operates under terms consistent with the gift principle.
