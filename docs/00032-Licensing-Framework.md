## 1. Purpose of this document

This document describes the framework within which UniCORE AI is published. It explains how three separate instruments work together:

1. The legal copyright licence in `LICENSE.md` (CC BY 4.0).
2. The Brand and Trademark Use Policy in `BRAND-AND-TRADEMARK-USE-POLICY.md`.
3. The architectural principles documented in this repository (the 12-Level model, the TrueAI Foundation invariants, the Inter-Level Messaging Protocol, the Human Override Protocol, the Reasonable Governance Threshold).

This document is companion material. It is not itself a licence. The binding legal terms are in `LICENSE.md`.

## 2. The three instruments

### 2.1 Copyright licence

The architectural content of UniCORE AI is licensed under **Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International** (CC BY 4.0). This is a real and irrevocable gift. Any person may use, modify, redistribute, and build upon the work for any purpose, including commercial purposes, provided that attribution is preserved, changes are indicated, the licence is linked, and no endorsement is claimed.

The licence is the operative legal instrument. Anything in this or any other companion document that contradicts the licence is non-binding; the licence prevails.

### 2.2 Trademark and brand protection

The names "UniVERSE", "TrueAI", "UniCORE AI", "TrueAI Foundation", and "UniCORE Foundation" are protected by trademark. Their use to identify the canonical work, or to claim compliance, certification, or endorsement, is governed by the Brand and Trademark Use Policy.

Trademark is the appropriate enforcement vehicle for the names and the official endorsements. CC BY 4.0 cannot restrict commercial use of the architectural content; trademark law can restrict commercial use of the names. The two instruments are complementary.

### 2.3 Architectural principles

The 12-Level model, the TrueAI Foundation invariants, the Inter-Level Messaging Protocol, the Human Override Protocol, and the Reasonable Governance Threshold are the architectural substance of UniCORE AI. They are licensed under CC BY 4.0 like the rest of the work. They are not legally immutable; CC BY 4.0 permits derivatives.

Their **canonical form** is held by Unitek Systems Limited as custodian, and is reproduced in the canonical repositories. A derivative work that modifies these components is a different work; it is not entitled to be called UniCORE AI or to claim the certification that goes with that name. That restriction is enforced by trademark, not by the copyright licence.

## 3. What this framework asks of users

This section is guidance rather than legal restriction. Departure from this guidance is not a breach of the licence; it is a departure from the authors' intent, and the authors reserve the right to publicly note such departures and to withhold endorsement.

### 3.1 Preserve attribution

Carry the attribution required by CC BY 4.0. Link to the canonical repository. Indicate any changes you have made. Do not strip authorship.

### 3.2 Use the names accurately

Refer to your work as "UniCORE AI" only when it implements the canonical architecture without modification of the immutable components. If you modify the immutable components, name your work distinctly. The Brand and Trademark Use Policy governs the use of the names in detail.

### 3.3 Honour the human-sovereignty principles

The architecture is built on the premise that human authority remains primary. Implementations that remove human override, that introduce autonomous self-modification, or that route around the Reasonable Governance Threshold depart from the architecture's intent. The licence does not forbid such implementations; the authors do not endorse them.

### 3.4 Maintain a transparent chain of authorship

If your work is a derivative, say so. Identify what you changed. Make the modification visible so that downstream users can trace the lineage. This is good practice in CC BY 4.0 ecosystems and is requested here as a matter of community norm.

### 3.5 Do not weaponise the architecture

The authors ask that the architecture not be used to build systems whose primary purpose is to harm humans, suppress human authority, or operate outside human oversight. This is a moral ask, not a legal restriction. Users who depart from it will not be endorsed by Unitek Systems Limited or the Foundations.

## 4. What the licence permits

Under CC BY 4.0, the following are permitted without permission and without payment:

- Commercial use by any party in any sector.
- Commercial derivative works.
- Inclusion in closed-source or proprietary products, provided attribution is preserved and the licence link is provided.
- Forks under different open licences.
- Use by governments, agencies, research institutions, and private companies anywhere in the world.
- Use in regulated sectors including banking, healthcare, law, and public safety.
- Use in space, defence, and security applications, subject to applicable export and dual-use laws (which are not modified by this licence).

The licence does not gate any of the above.

## 5. What the licence does not permit

Under CC BY 4.0:

- Attribution may not be removed or falsified.
- The licence link and the indication-of-changes requirement must be preserved.
- A user may not claim that the licensor endorses their particular use or derivative.

That is the full extent of the licence's restrictions on use.

## 6. What additional restrictions apply

Restrictions beyond the licence come from separate instruments and from general law:

- **Trademark law and the Brand and Trademark Use Policy** restrict use of the names, logos, and certification phrases.
- **Passing-off and misrepresentation law** restrict false attribution and false claims of endorsement.
- **Export control, dual-use, and sanctions law** apply to applications in defence, security, and certain regulated sectors.
- **Data protection law** (UK GDPR, US sector-specific frameworks, and equivalents) applies to any implementation that processes personal data.
- **Sector-specific regulation** (financial services, healthcare, legal practice) applies as it would to any other software or governance framework.

This document does not modify any of those external regimes.

## 7. Custodian role

Unitek Systems Limited is the custodian of the canonical work. Its custodian role includes:

- Maintaining the canonical repositories.
- Registering and enforcing trademarks on the programme names.
- Publishing and maintaining this framework, the Brand and Trademark Use Policy, and related governance documents.
- Responding to misuse, passing-off, and false-endorcement actions through trademark enforcement and general law.

Unitek Systems Limited does not licence the architectural content commercially. The architectural content is already commercially usable under CC BY 4.0 without permission from Unitek Systems Limited.

## 8. Foundation oversight

The TrueAI Foundation and the UniCORE Foundation, once constituted, will be independent of Unitek Systems Limited. Their role is to oversee the ecosystem of producers building on the architecture. They are not commercial licensing bodies. They may publish certification schemes, recommend conformance criteria, and disburse funds to support the ecosystem. They do not own the licence.
