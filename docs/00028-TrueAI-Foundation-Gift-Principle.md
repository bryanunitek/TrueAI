## 1. The principle

The TrueAI Foundation and UniCORE AI are **given, not sold**.

This is a first-class principle of the architecture. It is the reason the work is released under the most permissive Creative Commons licence available with attribution preserved (CC BY 4.0), and the reason that no commercial licensing channel is offered or required to use the work.

The principle applies to:

- the TrueAI Foundation itself
- UniCORE AI as a whole
- the UniVERSE vision (the wider programme of AI, TrueAI, and UniCORE AI)
- all governance documents in the public repositories (the TrueAI Constitution, the Reasonable Governance Threshold, the Human Override Protocol, the 12-Level Model, the country/state/tax/compliance/mission governance documents)
- all architectural diagrams, specifications, and reference materials in the public repositories
- the prototype implementation work where it has been published

## 2. What "given" means in this work

The work is given under CC BY 4.0. CC BY 4.0 is the legal instrument. It grants any party the right to use, modify, redistribute, and build upon the work, including for commercial purposes, irrevocably and worldwide, on the sole conditions that attribution is preserved, changes are indicated, the licence is linked, and no claim of endorsement is made.

That is the operative gift. It is real and it is legally binding.

This document is the moral framing that sits behind the choice of licence. The moral framing explains why CC BY 4.0 was chosen. It does not add legal restrictions beyond what CC BY 4.0 contains.

## 3. Why given, not sold

### 3.1 Truth cannot be owned

A foundation whose purpose is to anchor artificial intelligence to truth cannot itself be gated by commercial ownership. The moment truth becomes a commercial asset, truth is distorted by commercial interest.

### 3.2 Governance cannot be owned

A governance foundation that humanity may adopt at a civilisational scale must be free of any single owner's control. Owned governance is not governance — it is influence.

### 3.3 Safety must not be paywalled

Safety-critical architecture must not be available only to those who can pay for it. Small governments, research institutions, public hospitals, and long-duration missions must have the same access as the largest corporations.

### 3.4 The 100-year horizon requires independence

For UniCORE AI to remain viable for a century, it cannot depend on the survival of any single company or the continuation of any single business model. CC BY 4.0 outlasts the company that released the work.

### 3.5 An irrevocable licence cannot be revoked

Once granted under CC BY 4.0, the right to use the work is perpetual and irrevocable. A future custodian cannot withdraw the licence. A sold product can be discontinued, raised in price, closed-sourced, or weaponised by withdrawal. An irrevocable gift under CC BY 4.0 cannot be.

## 4. What "given" means operationally

### 4.1 Public by default

Every repository carrying the TrueAI Foundation, UniCORE AI, or UniVERSE work is public from the moment of publication. There is no private phase, no paid tier, no "released later" phase, no "premium" tier.

### 4.2 Open licence

The work is released under CC BY 4.0. The licence file in the repository root is the operative legal instrument. There is no second-tier or paid-tier licence; CC BY 4.0 is the only licence and it grants the full set of rights it grants.

### 4.3 Commercial use permitted

Under CC BY 4.0, commercial use of the architecture is permitted without permission and without payment. Any party may build commercial products, services, or implementations on this work, including in regulated sectors, including as closed-source derivatives, subject only to the attribution requirements of the licence.

This is intentional. A gift that cannot be used commercially is not a gift to producers building real systems in real sectors. The gift exists so that those producers can build.

### 4.4 Immediate publication

The work is published now, not after commercialisation attempts. There is no business model that delays the gift. Each version is published as soon as it is ready.

### 4.5 Mirrored globally

To ensure that no single party can frustrate access to the work, the authors encourage independent mirroring across public repositories, academic archives, agency archives, and national preservation systems. Attribution requirements support mirroring; they do not impede it.

### 4.6 Attribution, not ownership

Attribution requirements ("Powered by UniCORE AI", "Built on the TrueAI Foundation", link to the TrueAI Foundation MD file) exist to preserve traceability and integrity. They do not assert commercial ownership over derivative works.

## 5. What "given" does not mean

### 5.1 It does not mean that all implementation work must be free

The licence covers the architectural content of the work. It does not require that anyone who implements the architecture must do so for free. Enterprises may charge for:

- their own implementation work
- integration services
- training
- support contracts
- extensions
- closed-source products built on the architecture, provided attribution is preserved

Under CC BY 4.0 such commercial activity is fully permitted. The gift is the architecture; what producers build with it is their own work to charge for or not.

### 5.2 It does not mean that the names are free for any use

The names "UniVERSE", "TrueAI", "UniCORE AI", "TrueAI Foundation", and "UniCORE Foundation" are protected by trademark. Their use to identify the canonical work, to claim compliance or certification, or to assert endorsement, is governed by the Brand and Trademark Use Policy. Commercial implementations built on the architecture must name their own products and may refer to the architecture by name in factual descriptive use; they may not adopt the names as if their work were the canonical version.

This is the principal lever Unitek Systems Limited holds to protect the integrity of the work. The licence cannot restrict commercial use; trademark can restrict commercial use of the names. The two instruments work together.

### 5.3 It does not mean that the authors endorse all uses

Under CC BY 4.0 the authors do not gate use. They also do not endorse all use. Implementations that depart from the human-sovereignty principles, that remove human override, that introduce autonomous self-modification, or that weaponise the architecture, are not breaches of the licence. They are uses the authors do not endorse, and the authors retain the right to say so publicly.

### 5.4 It does not mean no structure

Attribution, licence linkage, indication of changes, and the Brand and Trademark Use Policy all apply. "Given" means freely usable, not unregulated. The regulation that exists is in the trademark instrument and the licence's attribution requirement, not in restrictions on commercial use.

## 6. Relationship to the licence

The Gift Principle is the moral framing. CC BY 4.0 (in `LICENSE.md`) is the legal instrument. The Brand and Trademark Use Policy is the second-instrument enforcement vehicle for the names and certifications.

If anything in this document appears to add restrictions beyond the licence, the licence prevails. The licence is what is given; this document explains the spirit in which it is given.

## 7. Why this is recorded now

Once UniCORE AI gains attention from governments, from AI companies, from investors, there will be pressure to:

- create a commercial version with restricted features
- retain premium components privately
- delay public release for strategic advantage
- offer exclusive licensing to specific partners
- carve out exceptions

This document records the principle before the pressure begins. It states that the authors chose CC BY 4.0 deliberately, with the consequences fully accepted, and that no negotiation can quietly remove the irrevocable rights already granted to all recipients.

CC BY 4.0 itself supports this: once granted, the rights cannot be revoked. The principle and the legal instrument reinforce each other.

## 8. Violations

The licence has its own breach conditions: failure to preserve attribution, failure to indicate changes, failure to link to the licence, and false claims of endorsement. Where any of these occur, the relevant remedies under CC BY 4.0 and general law are available.

Beyond the licence, the principal enforcement vehicle is the Brand and Trademark Use Policy. Misuse of the names, false certification claims, and passing-off are addressed through trademark and general law.

There is no separate "Gift Principle" enforcement mechanism that operates outside these regimes. The principle is real, the framing is binding morally, the legal instruments are CC BY 4.0 and trademark.

## 9. Summary

UniCORE AI and the TrueAI Foundation are given to humanity under CC BY 4.0. The gift is real, irrevocable, and commercially usable by anyone. The names and certifications are separately protected by trademark. The authors do not endorse all uses, and reserve the right to publicly note departures from the architecture's intent.

It is given — because truth, governance, and safety at civilisational scale cannot be sold.
