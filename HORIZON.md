# HORIZON

**Time horizon, readiness, and what this programme is not yet.**

Author: Bryan Fred, Unitek Systems Limited, Bedford, United Kingdom
Status: Public. Given, not sold. Irrevocable.
First published: 2026-05-13

---

## What is ready today

- The **TrueAI Foundation** invariants are stable, published, and citable. They are usable now as a reference standard against which any AI system can be assessed.
- The **UniCORE AI** reference architecture — the 12-level governance stack — is published in full.
- The **UniVERSE** programme materials — whitepapers, founding statement, governance model — are published.

These are the written-down artefacts. They exist. They are open for challenge, translation, adaptation, and implementation under CC BY 4.0.

## What is not ready today

No known AI system currently satisfies all of the TrueAI Foundation invariants at production conformance. That includes the prototype systems being built by the author.

**This is expected.** Governance foundations are not proven at launch. They are proven by prolonged use under real-world stress.

## Time horizon

The programme's time-to-maturity is measured in decades, not quarters. A realistic horizon:

- First partial implementations reaching production use in narrow domains: **1–3 years**.
- First implementations demonstrating full conformance to the invariants under independent audit: **5–10 years**.
- The programme reaching the maturity that would let it function as a genuine civilisational governance layer: **10–20 years, possibly longer**.

This is not a delay. It is the nature of the work.

For comparison: Underwriters Laboratories has been certifying electrical safety for over 130 years. The Kitemark has been operating for over 120 years. Fairtrade for over 35. Governance and certification frameworks become trustworthy by testing across many systems, many jurisdictions, many adversaries, and many edge cases, over many years. There is no path that shortcuts that.

## Why publish now

Two reasons.

First, the invariants themselves are stable and ready to be cited, challenged, extended, and adopted as a reference. The long clock does not start running until the written foundation exists. Publication is how that clock starts.

Second, this is the author's considered view of where humanity will need to be in 10–20 years in its relationship with artificial intelligence. The programme exists because that future is coming whether it is prepared for or not. Publishing the foundation now is an act of preparation, not an act of shipping a product.

## What this means for readers

- **If you are looking for production-ready software right now:** this is not that. Implementations exist and are being built openly, but they are prototypes under active development, not shipped products.
- **If you are looking for a reference standard to assess existing or planned AI systems against:** the invariants are ready now. Use them.
- **If you are looking to contribute:** challenge the invariants in [Discussions](https://github.com/bryanunitek/TrueAI/discussions), translate them, adapt them, extend them under CC BY 4.0, or build implementations. All welcome.
- **If you are hoping the author will monetise this:** the programme is given, not sold. That is not a future plan that might change. It is structural. See [LICENSE.md](LICENSE.md) and [STATEMENT-ON-CLAIMS.md](STATEMENT-ON-CLAIMS.md).
