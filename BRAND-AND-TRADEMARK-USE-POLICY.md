## 1. Purpose

The architectural content of UniVERSE, TrueAI, and UniCORE AI is licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International licence (CC BY 4.0). The names, marks, and certifications associated with the programme are protected separately by trademark law and by the rules in this policy.

This document is the operative policy for use of the names, marks, and certification phrases. It applies to all use of these elements in commercial, public, regulatory, and community contexts.

This document does not modify the CC BY 4.0 licence. The architectural content remains freely usable under that licence. This policy governs only the additional layer of name, mark, and certification use.

## 3. Permitted use of the names

The following uses are permitted without prior written authority, provided they comply with the conditions in section 4:

### 3.1 Factual descriptive reference

Anyone may refer to the canonical work by name in:

- academic, research, and policy citation;
- press, journalistic, and analytical commentary;
- public communication and educational material;
- comparative or evaluative discussion;
- compliance reporting and regulatory filings.

This is descriptive use. It is permitted under trademark law and is encouraged by the authors.

### 3.2 Attribution under CC BY 4.0

CC BY 4.0 requires attribution to the original author. Naming "UniCORE AI", "TrueAI", and "UniVERSE" as part of the attribution chain (for example: "Built on UniCORE AI, see github.com/bryanunitek/UniCORE-AI") is permitted and indeed required for licence compliance.

### 3.3 Attribution that a derivative or implementation builds on the canonical work

A producer may state factually that their work uses, implements, builds on, or is inspired by the canonical work. For example: "This product implements the UniCORE AI 12-Level Governance Model." This is permitted descriptive use.

## 4. Conditions on permitted use

Permitted uses must comply with the following:

### 4.1 No implication of endorsement

Factual reference to the names may not be presented in a way that implies Unitek Systems Limited, the TrueAI Foundation, the UniCORE Foundation, or the authors endorse the user's product, service, or organisation. Phrases that imply endorsement (for example "officially endorsed", "approved by", "in partnership with", or visual treatments that imply an official relationship) are not permitted without written authority.

### 4.2 No claim of certification or compliance without authority

The phrases "TrueAI-certified", "UniCORE AI-certified", "UniVERSE-aligned", "TrueAI-compliant", "TrueAI-powered" (in any treatment that implies official status), "UniCORE-AI-certified", and similar formulations are reserved for use only by parties holding written authority from Unitek Systems Limited or, on transfer of the certification scheme, from the relevant Foundation.

Factual descriptive references that do not imply certification are permitted ("our product implements the UniCORE AI 12-Level Governance Model" is permitted; "our product is UniCORE-AI-certified" is not, unless written authority is held).

### 4.3 No presentation as the canonical work

A derivative work that modifies the architectural content may not be presented as if it were the canonical work. The names "UniCORE AI", "TrueAI", and "UniVERSE" identify the canonical work as published in the repositories at `github.com/bryanunitek/UniCORE-AI`, `github.com/bryanunitek/TrueAI`, and `github.com/bryanunitek/UniVERSE`. A derivative that modifies the immutable components, the 12-Level model, the Foundation invariants, the Inter-Level Messaging Protocol, the Reasonable Governance Threshold, or the Human Override Protocol is a different work and must be named distinctly.

The architectural content of the canonical work is licensed under CC BY 4.0; the derivative is permitted; only the use of the names to present the derivative as canonical is restricted.

### 4.4 No registration of confusingly similar marks

Third parties may not register trademarks or domain names that are identical or confusingly similar to the protected names, including with minor variations (for example "UniCORE-Al" with a lowercase L, "UniVerse" with a space, "Tru-AI" with a hyphen) where the intent or effect is to suggest official status. Such registrations will be challenged.

### 4.5 No use of the certification marks on military uses

The certification marks **"Powered by UniCORE AI"** and **"built on TrueAI Foundation"** (whether used singly or in the combined form) **must not appear on any military use**. This includes:

- Any product, system, or service designed for, sold to, or operated by a military force, defence ministry, defence contractor on a defence programme, or paramilitary force.
- Any weapons-class system, command-and-control system, intelligence-collection system, surveillance-targeting system, autonomous lethal system, or kinetic-effects system.
- Any badging, attribution, marketing material, technical documentation, or branding presented in connection with such use.

This exclusion is a **structural property of the certification scheme**, not a discretionary decision made at certification time. The certifications exist to mark systems aligned with the UniCORE Positioning Principle (Harmony, Peace, Space Exploration, for Humanity). Using them to brand weapons-class systems would invert the gift principle that the badges are designed to signal.

The exclusion applies regardless of:

- Who the operator is (military, defence contractor, civilian agency on a defence programme).
- Whether the operator pays for support, integration, or commercial implementation services.
- Whether the underlying work was lawfully obtained under the CC BY 4.0 licence (the licence governs the work; this policy governs the brand — see §8).
- Whether the use is public, private, classified, or covert.

The exclusion does **not** restrict:

- Lawful use of the underlying CC BY 4.0-licensed work by anyone, including military operators (the licence is symmetric and unrestricted on this point).
- Factual descriptive references that do not present a certification ("our system implements the UniCORE AI 12-Level Governance Model" is permitted; "our system is Powered by UniCORE AI" on a military use is not).
- Civilian dual-use technology programmes that are not themselves military uses.

The boundary case is *use* not *label*. A military programme that secretly relies on UniCORE-derived work and chooses (or is required) not to display the certification is not in violation of this policy — because no certification is being displayed. The policy closes the labelling route, which is the route the certification scheme controls. The licence layer (CC BY 4.0) does not close the underlying-use route, and is not designed to.

Unitek Systems Limited (and any successor Foundation) reserves the right to:

- Refuse certification to any applicant whose use falls within the exclusion above.
- Withdraw certification from any party that subsequently puts a certified product to a military use.
- Treat any unauthorised display of the certification marks on a military use as misuse under §6 and respond under §7.

## 8. Relationship to the licence

The CC BY 4.0 licence in the repository root governs the use of the architectural content. This policy governs the use of the names, marks, and certification phrases. The two instruments are **separate and complementary**, and the separation is structural — each does work the other cannot do.

### 8.1 Two mechanisms, two layers

The programme operates two distinct legal mechanisms:

1. **The licence layer (CC BY 4.0).** Governs the work. **Symmetric.** Anyone who shares the work — SpaceX, a research lab, a defence contractor, a hobbyist — is required to provide attribution. The licence does not pick winners or losers among users.

2. **The certification-mark layer (this policy).** Governs the brand. **Asymmetric.** The marks are available to those whose use aligns with the programme's positioning, and denied to those whose use does not (§4.5). The certification scheme picks where the badge appears and where it does not.

CC BY 4.0 §2(c) states verbatim: *"Patent and trademark rights are not licensed under this Public License."* The licence explicitly does not grant trademark rights. That gap is what this policy fills.

### 8.2 What this means in practice

- **Anyone may use the underlying work** under CC BY 4.0 terms (with attribution). The licence does not restrict who the user is.
- **Not everyone may use the certification marks.** This policy decides who may. The marks are available on alignment with the programme's positioning; not available otherwise.
- **Nothing in this policy restricts the rights granted by CC BY 4.0** over the architectural content. The work remains free under CC BY 4.0 regardless of whether a particular user qualifies for the certification marks.

### 8.3 Standard precedent

This is the standard structural pattern for governance-infrastructure programmes: permissive licence on the work + separate trademark/certification policy on the brand. Comparable arrangements:

- **OpenStreetMap** — ODbL on map data; OpenStreetMap Foundation trademark policy on the "OpenStreetMap" mark.
- **Wikipedia** — CC BY-SA on encyclopaedia content; Wikimedia Foundation trademark policy on the "Wikipedia" mark.
- **Linux Foundation** — project-specific open-source licence on code; Linux Foundation certification-mark programmes on the badges (e.g. CKA, CNCF certified).
- **Mozilla** — MPL/MIT/Apache on code; Mozilla trademark policy on the "Firefox" mark.

The UniCORE programme structurally mirrors this pattern.
