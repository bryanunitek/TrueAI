## 1. Purpose

The architectural content of UniVERSE, TrueAI, and UniCORE AI is licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International licence (CC BY 4.0). The names, marks, and certifications associated with the programme are protected separately by trademark law and by the rules in this policy.

This document is the operative policy for use of the names, marks, and certification phrases. It applies to all use of these elements in commercial, public, regulatory, and community contexts.

This document does not modify the CC BY 4.0 licence. The architectural content remains freely usable under that licence. This policy governs only the additional layer of name, mark, and certification use.

## 2. Protected names and marks

The following names and marks are claimed as trademarks of Unitek Systems Limited (or, on transfer, of the relevant programme subsidiary or Foundation):

- **UniVERSE** — the wider programme name.
- **TrueAI** — the foundation name.
- **TrueAI Foundation** — the Foundation entity name.
- **UniCORE AI** — the architecture name.
- **UniCORE Foundation** — the Foundation entity name.
- **Generation IT** — the producer-pool concept name.

Registration is in progress in the United Kingdom and the United States. The marks are claimed by use from May 2026 onwards.

## 3. Permitted use of the names

The following uses are permitted without prior written authority, provided they comply with the conditions in section 4:

### 3.1 Factual descriptive reference

Anyone may refer to the canonical work by name in:

- academic, research, and policy citation;
- press, journalistic, and analytical commentary;
- public communication and educational material;
- comparative or evaluative discussion;
- compliance reporting and regulatory filings.

This is descriptive use. It is permitted under trademark law and is encouraged by the authors.

### 3.2 Attribution under CC BY 4.0

CC BY 4.0 requires attribution to the original author. Naming "UniCORE AI", "TrueAI", and "UniVERSE" as part of the attribution chain (for example: "Built on UniCORE AI, see github.com/bryanunitek/UniCORE-AI") is permitted and indeed required for licence compliance.

### 3.3 Attribution that a derivative or implementation builds on the canonical work

A producer may state factually that their work uses, implements, builds on, or is inspired by the canonical work. For example: "This product implements the UniCORE AI 12-Level Governance Model." This is permitted descriptive use.

## 4. Conditions on permitted use

Permitted uses must comply with the following:

### 4.1 No implication of endorsement

Factual reference to the names may not be presented in a way that implies Unitek Systems Limited, the TrueAI Foundation, the UniCORE Foundation, or the authors endorse the user's product, service, or organisation. Phrases that imply endorsement (for example "officially endorsed", "approved by", "in partnership with", or visual treatments that imply an official relationship) are not permitted without written authority.

### 4.2 No claim of certification or compliance without authority

The phrases "TrueAI-certified", "UniCORE AI-certified", "UniVERSE-aligned", "TrueAI-compliant", "TrueAI-powered" (in any treatment that implies official status), "UniCORE-AI-certified", and similar formulations are reserved for use only by parties holding written authority from Unitek Systems Limited or, on transfer of the certification scheme, from the relevant Foundation.

Factual descriptive references that do not imply certification are permitted ("our product implements the UniCORE AI 12-Level Governance Model" is permitted; "our product is UniCORE-AI-certified" is not, unless written authority is held).

### 4.3 No presentation as the canonical work

A derivative work that modifies the architectural content may not be presented as if it were the canonical work. The names "UniCORE AI", "TrueAI", and "UniVERSE" identify the canonical work as published in the repositories at `github.com/bryanunitek/UniCORE-AI`, `github.com/bryanunitek/TrueAI`, and `github.com/bryanunitek/UniVERSE`. A derivative that modifies the immutable components, the 12-Level model, the Foundation invariants, the Inter-Level Messaging Protocol, the Reasonable Governance Threshold, or the Human Override Protocol is a different work and must be named distinctly.

The architectural content of the canonical work is licensed under CC BY 4.0; the derivative is permitted; only the use of the names to present the derivative as canonical is restricted.

### 4.4 No registration of confusingly similar marks

Third parties may not register trademarks or domain names that are identical or confusingly similar to the protected names, including with minor variations (for example "UniCORE-Al" with a lowercase L, "UniVerse" with a space, "Tru-AI" with a hyphen) where the intent or effect is to suggest official status. Such registrations will be challenged.

## 5. Authority to grant rights beyond this policy

Written authority to use the names beyond what this policy permits may be granted by Unitek Systems Limited for the present. On transfer of the certification schemes to the Foundations, written authority for certification claims will be granted by the relevant Foundation.

Requests for written authority should be sent to Unitek Systems Limited at the address on the public repositories or by issue on the relevant GitHub repository.

## 6. Enforcement

This policy is enforced through:

- Trademark law in the relevant jurisdictions (the United Kingdom, the United States, and other jurisdictions as registration extends).
- Passing-off law and equivalent unfair-competition law where applicable.
- Misrepresentation law where false claims of endorsement or certification are made.
- General contract and tort law where the surrounding context creates obligations or causes harm.

CC BY 4.0 attribution failures are addressed under that licence's terms; this policy does not duplicate those mechanisms.

## 7. Public response to misuse

In addition to legal enforcement, Unitek Systems Limited reserves the right to publicly note misuse, false certification claims, and passing-off, on the canonical repositories and elsewhere, so that the public can distinguish authorised from unauthorised use.

## 8. Relationship to the licence

The CC BY 4.0 licence in the repository root governs the use of the architectural content. This policy governs the use of the names, marks, and certification phrases. The two instruments are separate and complementary. Nothing in this policy restricts the rights granted by CC BY 4.0 over the architectural content.

## 9. Updates to this policy

This policy may be updated. Updates apply prospectively. Past uses that complied with the policy in force at the time remain compliant. The current version is the one in the repository root at the date of access.
