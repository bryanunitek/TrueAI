# Discussions — TrueAI

**What this repository's Discussions tab is for, what it is not for, and how to take part.**

TrueAI is the foundation layer of the UniVERSE programme — the immutable principles that define what an AI system is permitted to be. The Discussions tab is where those principles are debated, questioned, and stress-tested in public. This page explains how to use the space well.

## What Discussions are for

Discussions on this repository are the right place for:

- **Challenging an invariant.** TrueAI is written in the form of absolute properties: truth-anchoring, non-self-modification, bounded behaviour, named human authority, reversibility, and the prohibition on an AI system acting on its human operators. If you think an invariant is wrong, missing, or insufficient, make the case here.
- **Edge-case analysis.** "How does invariant X apply when the system is doing Y?" is exactly the kind of question that makes the foundation stronger. Bring the concrete scenario, bring the reasoning.
- **Translation and adaptation.** The foundation is released under CC BY 4.0 so that it can be translated into other languages and adapted to other jurisdictions' terminology. If you are doing this, share the work.
- **Cross-references.** If you are using TrueAI invariants in academic work, policy material, or product governance, a Discussion thread helps others find your work and cite it.
- **Pointers and corrections.** Typos, contradictions, unclear phrasing: raise them here. The foundation is meant to be legible, and legibility is a community property.

## What Discussions are not for

- **Commercial solicitation.** TrueAI is a gift, not a product. Please do not pitch services, partnerships, or sales here.
- **Bug reports against downstream software.** TrueAI is a set of principles, not a piece of code. Implementation bugs belong in the implementing project's own repository.
- **Private or confidential material.** Discussions are public, permanent, and indexed. Use the private contact route for anything confidential.
- **Generic AI-ethics debate unrelated to the invariants.** There are many places online for general AI-ethics discussion. This space is specifically for the foundation.

## How to start a Discussion

- Pick the category that best fits your topic. If unsure, use **General**.
- Give the thread a direct, specific title — name the invariant or scenario in the title if you can.
- Link to the specific invariant or document section when you are citing it.
- Assume good faith. Disagree rigorously but respectfully.

## How responses work

- Bryan Fred (the author) reads Discussions activity regularly and may respond in person. Threads may also be picked up by other contributors. Silence is not dismissal; the author curates his responses.
- Discussions are moderated. Hostile, spammy, or bad-faith posts will be removed without notice.
- Responses to principle-level debate reflect the author's own view. TrueAI's invariants are published as a dated, attributed contribution; they are open to challenge, and the author will engage with serious challenge.

## Code of conduct

Participate the way you would want others to participate in a project you care about:

- Disagree with the substance, not the person.
- No doxxing, harassment, or targeting.
- No content that violates applicable law or another party's rights.
